Live online seminars · Grades 4–12

Where kids read real books—deeply—and grow into real writers.

Students read demanding full-length books about two grade levels early, mark them up by hand, and defend their thinking in small, live online seminars. Between seminars, an AI engine reads what they wrote and what they marked—and gives feedback specific enough to drive a real revision.

Why it matters

Few childhood habits shape a whole life like reading well.

When machines can read and write for us, doing it yourself becomes the rarer, more valuable skill—and the payoff isn't just better English grades. Decades of research tie a strong, advanced reading habit to outcomes that follow a child for the rest of their life:

Better gradesAcross every subject, not just English.
Higher IQReading skill is linked to gains in verbal—and even nonverbal—ability.
Higher incomeLiteracy tracks strongly with lifetime earnings.
Better quality of lifeStronger wellbeing and health—studies even link book-reading to living longer.
Sharper communicationA larger vocabulary and clearer, more persuasive expression.

Associations from decades of published research; reading is a major contributing factor, not the only one.

Reading rewires attention

Finishing a demanding book trains the sustained focus that scrolling quietly erodes—a workout for the part of the mind school rarely exercises.

Writing is thinking

You don't fully know what you think until you try to write it clearly. Building and defending an argument is the skill hiding underneath every other academic one.

It compounds

Strong readers and writers do better across every subject, in admissions, and at work. Few advantages start this early or last this long.

The evidence

Not our opinion—the research.

Two findings that shape how we teach: the more children read, the higher they tend to score—and a lifelong reading habit is linked to a longer, healthier life.

How a week works

One weekly loop. Humans do the thinking work; AI does the grind.

Every week runs the same rhythm. The parts that need a person—discussion, judgment, mentorship—stay human. The tireless feedback-and-revision grind, historically too expensive to do well, is where the AI earns its keep.

01Human

Read & annotate

02AI

03Human

Live seminar

6–10 students defend their reading out loud with a credentialed instructor. Un-fakeable, and the heart of the program.

Grade & portfolio

Holistic human grading, a mastery-ladder check, and one more entry in the portfolio parents watch grow.

      The curriculum

      Two ladders: harder books, and a real progression of writing.

      Reading runs about two grade levels ahead. Writing climbs a public mastery ladder—so "your child passed Level 6: rhetorical analysis" means something specific and earned.

      The reading ladder

      Whole books across fiction and non-fiction—novels, memoir, narrative non-fiction, essays, and speeches—read ~2 grades ahead. Representative titles; the working list is set with the instructor.

      The writing mastery ladder

      Each rung is a form to master before the next. The engine scores every draft against the rung it's on.

      • Level 1Narrative
      • Level 2Descriptive
      • Level 3Expository
      • Level 4Literary response
      • Level 5Persuasive / argument
      • Level 6Rhetorical analysis
      • Level 7Research & synthesis

      Alongside both ladders we build reading fluency and pace flexibility—knowing when to skim, when to cruise, and when to slow right down. Not speed-reading for its own sake, but the stamina that lets a student take on more and read the hard parts closely.

      Questions parents ask

      The honest answers.

      Won't AI just let my child cheat?
      It's the opposite of the risk. The live seminar is un-fakeable—your child has to defend their reading out loud, in front of peers, in real time. And we make the writing process observable: we keep draft history and a private sense of how your child writes, so a draft that doesn't match can be flagged for a teacher to look at—never an automated accusation. We do not use commercial "AI detectors"; falsely accusing a child is a line we won't cross.
      How is this different from a worksheet or skills program?
      A worksheet drills a skill in isolation and moves on. We build readers: whole, demanding books read early, marked up by hand, and argued out loud—then real writing that climbs a mastery ladder. The difference shows up where it counts, in an independent reading-level check a few times a year—not in a stack of completed pages.
      How is this different from an AI tutor or chatbot?
      An AI-only tutor tries to replace the teacher. We do the reverse: the AI handles the instant feedback-and-revision grind so a credentialed instructor can spend their time on discussion, judgment, and mentorship. The part that actually forms a reader—the seminar—is always human. The AI is a lever for the teacher, never a substitute for one.
      What's included for $245 a month?
      Small, live online seminars (6–10 students), whole-book deep reading, a daily revision loop with real feedback, and a credentialed instructor who comes to know your child. Reading level is checked by an independent assessment through the year, so progress is something you can see—not a number we grade ourselves.
      Does this replace our math program?
      No—it's the complement. Many of our families already have math handled by a program they love. Think of us as the reading-and-writing counterpart: that program for math, us for reading and writing.
